Tips 'n Tricks

When sailing, you are eventually going to get lost.
If you do get lost (or wanna prevent getting lost), then this is the page for you.
I will show you just a few of the tools I used in my time as a sailor.

Tips

Compass:
There is a magnetized needle inside, aligned to the Earth's magnetic poles.
Because of that, it will always point north.
Watch a video on how to use it:


Astrolabe: An astrolabe uses a fixed point in the sky (such as the sun) and starsf
to tell the time and latitude.
Here's how to use it:


https://electronics.howstuffworks.com/gadgets/clocks-watches/astrolabe.htm#:~:text=Hold%20the%20rope%20so%20that,by%20looking%20directly%20at%20it.

Hourglass: An hourglass has a powder in it, typically sand. The sand flows through the thin tube,
taking time for the full thing to go through. Some hourglasses take 5 minutes, other hours.
These are used to measure the time.

Tricks

  • Look at the horizon. Don't go sailing in storms
  • Practice good creativity. Practice it cause you might find a new land mass! When I did I named it 'New Found Land' and that isn't very creative (look I was young and stupid alright!?).
  • When looking for a sponsor, choose one that does not require you to change your name. John Cabot doesn't have the same ring as Giovanni Caboto.
  • Do not ascend to immortality. You get bored quickly. On second thought... achieve immortality, then we can play God of War together...
